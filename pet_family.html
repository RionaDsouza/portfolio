❝ Most people who want to adopt pets don't have the right platform to do so. ❞


Project Overview

An online portal gives users the options to view different animals and decide which one would be the right fit for them. Pet Family is a website that streamlines the process of pet adoption. A lot of people who want to adopt pets cannot do so due to lack of options. This website will solve that problem by giving users the option to choose a pet based on their preferences.


Project Duration

I completed this project of the Google certificate. It took me about a month to complete it. As this was the first time I was designing for multiple screen sizes, I took my time with the whole process.


My Roles

sole UX designer, UX researcher


My Responsibilities

research, ideation, wireframing, prototyping, responsive design, testing, iterating on designs


Understanding the User

User Stories

As a person who wants to get a dog I want to be able to look at dogs online and adopt one so that I don't have to spend time talking to people who take too long or sometimes don't even respond.

As a busy employee who wants to adopt a dog I want to be able to look at dogs and know their history before adopting them so that I know if my dog will be able to live with it.

User Research Summary

I knew of a few people who wanted to adopt a pet but couldn't find any. I conducted a survey to know exactly what people wanted from the app. I analysed the answers to come up with a solution that could help maximum users. From the survey answers, I built an empathy map to empathize with the users better.

User Pain Points

• There are very few adoptions websites in India.
• The existing websites have confusing navigation.

Ideation

Paper Wireframes

I sketched some wireframes on paper to get a clear idea of what I wanted the website to look like.
After some sketching and iterating, I can up with the optimal design for the home page and other pages as well.
I drew stars against the elements that I liked and wanted to include in the final draft of the wireframes.

Digital Wireframes

Once I had an idea about the layout of the website, I moved on to making digital wireframes of the same.
My goal was to make the navigation simple.
All the pages must be accessible from any part of the wesite.

Digital Wireframes for additional screen sizes

As I wanted Pet Family to be a responsive website, I worked on wireframes for a mobile screen size as well.

To create the low fidelity prototype I connected all the screens involved in the primary user flow for the adoption. I took feedback from the users about the flow of the website.

Usability Study Findings

There was no option to send a saved application.

Users wanted to view more than one photo of the animal on the website.

There was no confirmation page once the payment for donation was done.

Based on the feedback I received from the usability study, I refined my designs and made the required changes.

Accessibility Considerations

Impact

Through this project I aim to make the process of pet adoption simpler. A lot of people who want to adopt cannot do it because of lack of choices. With this website, they can get the opportunity to do the good that they want to.


What I Learnt

I learnt that even a small design change can have a huge impact on the overall user experience. The most important takeaway for me would be that design is not a singular task. It is a process of iterating and reiterating.
